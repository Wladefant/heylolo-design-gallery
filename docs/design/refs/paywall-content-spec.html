Paywall Content Spec — HeyLolo (corrected)

Why this exists. The live paywall ships template filler ("HeyLoLo Mastery Family · $19.99/month · 7 Day Free Trial", "Advanced prompting: build stories, apps, and creative worlds", "Priority support: 24/7 assistance", "300 Image Credits / month", the "fetures" typo). It describes a service we do not run, on a tier system we killed (#191), at a price and trial length that contradict the settled canon. This doc replaces it with copy that maps 1:1 onto the real, enforceable entitlement system and real service COGS.

Scope guardrails (do NOT reopen): pricing is settled — Family $9.99/mo · $59.99/yr, Plus $14.99/mo · $99.99/yr, 14-day no-card trial (canon Jul 5, MERGED-LAUNCH-CALENDAR "Settled pricing", teardown §B). This spec only fills in what each tier honestly promises and the words on the screen.

Author: research pass 2026-07-17. Owner of the build: #191 (FE plan-content.ts + BE feature-access.types.ts/.rules.ts).

0. The hard constraint that shapes everything: what the quota engine can actually enforce

The paywall may only promise what the backend can meter. The only enforceable knobs are in elumi-kids-api/functions/src/api/feature-access/feature-access.rules.ts — one UsageLimitRule = { limit: number | null; rollingWindowDays?: number } per feature:

Enum (SubscriptionFeatures) What it counts Unit Window
AddChild child profiles created count rolling rollingWindowDays (default 30) / billing period
ChildAiChat kid chat messages count same
ChildAiChatImages image generations count same
ParentAiChat parent-dashboard chat messages count same
RecognizedSpeech STT calls (speech recognized) count of calls same
SynthesizedSpeech TTS calls (speech synthesized) count of calls same

Two facts that kill the current copy and constrain the new copy:

  1. There is no "minutes" meter and no "credits" ledger. Voice is metered as number of STT/TTS calls, not minutes. So "15–20 min/day" (canon) and "300 Image Credits" (live screen) are both framings, not knobs. We enforce them by mapping to call-count caps (see §C). True minute-capping would need a new duration knob — flagged as an open item.
  2. rollingWindowDays is the only time control. Set it to 1 for a daily cap, omit it on paid tiers to bind the cap to the billing month (that is exactly how the current Adventurer/Mastery rules already behave). A daily cap and a monthly cap on the same feature would need a second rule — not supported today (open item).

Everything below is expressed in these units so it is shippable, not aspirational.

C. Quota recommendation + the arithmetic

COGS inputs (sourced):

  • Image: gemini-3.1-flash-lite-image ("Nano Banana 2 Lite"), the settled go-forward default, = $0.034 / image at 1K (FAST-IMAGE-MODEL-DOSSIER §5, live-smoke-verified in image-model-setting/BE-IMPLEMENTATION at ~3.5 s). Today's Imagen 4 Fast is $0.02 but is shut down 2026-08-17, so we cost against $0.034. (Each generated image also fires one cheap describeGeneratedImage vision pass ≈ <$0.001 — folded into rounding.)
  • STT (voice in): Chirp streaming ≈ $0.064 / min of recognized audio ($0.016/15 s). Google STT pricing
  • TTS (voice out): Gemini Flash TTS bills by output token at 25 tokens/sec of audio; $6/1M tok (Gemini 3.5 Flash TTS) to $20/1M tok (Gemini 3.1 Flash TTS, the currently-liked Zephyr voice). A 20 s reply = 500 tok = $0.003–$0.010. Google TTS pricing

Real usage anchor (prod-chat-audit/IMAGE-GEN-AUDIT.md): 27 generated images total across ~1 month and 5 heavy kids; the single heaviest kid = 19 images/month. So P90 image usage ≈ ≤20/month. Image gen is currently not even rate-limited in prod and has never blocked anyone.

C1. Image caps — recommended (monthly, bound to billing period)

Tier Recommended cap Max COGS/mo (cap × $0.034) % of price Covers P90 (≤20/mo)?
Trial 5 / day (rollingWindowDays:1) ≤ $0.34/day; ≤ $2.38 over 14 days n/a (free) yes, generously
Family $9.99 30 / month 30 × $0.034 = $1.02 10.2% yes (1.5× headroom)
Plus $14.99 60 / month 60 × $0.034 = $2.04 13.6% yes (3× headroom)

Why not a daily image cap on paid tiers: a daily cap of 10 = 300/month theoretical max = the exact 300 the founder rejected, and 300 × $0.034 = $10.20/mo > the entire $9.99 Family price. The monthly cap is what makes the margin safe and is honest ("30 a month"). Dropping the word "credits" entirely — it reads as a paid-currency the app doesn't have.

C2. Voice caps — recommended (daily, doubles as the wellness cap)

Voice is metered as call count, not minutes, and is the dominant variable cost. Map the canon "15–20 min/day" to a daily call cap:

  • One voice turn ≈ 1 STT call + 1 TTS reply. A 15–20 min turn-taking session ≈ ~40 turns/day.
  • Per-turn COGS ≈ STT (kid actually speaks a fraction of the session; 10–15 s ⇒ $0.01–0.016) + TTS ($0.003–0.010) + chat LLM tokens ($0.002) ≈ ~$0.02–0.03/turn.
Tier Recommended daily cap Framing Worst-case COGS if maxed every day Realistic (P90 usage)
Trial ~40 STT + 40 TTS / day "about 15–20 min of voice a day" ≤ $1.20/day × 14 = ~$17 (trial only) far less
Family $9.99 ~40 STT + 40 TTS / day (rollingWindowDays:1) "healthy 15–20 min/day" ~$0.80–1.20/day → up to ~$25–36/mo P90 well under $5/mo
Plus $14.99 ~60 STT + 60 TTS / day "~25 min/day" ~$1.20–1.80/day P90 under $8/mo

Honest margin finding the founder must see: at Google's list voice prices, a pathological kid who maxes the voice cap every single day can approach or exceed the monthly gross on Family. That is why the daily voice cap is non-negotiable margin protection, not just a wellness feature — and why we should NOT set voice to unlimited (limit: null) on a $9.99 tier. Two levers pull worst-case back down: (1) switch TTS to Gemini 3.5 Flash TTS ($6/1M) — ~70% cheaper than the 3.1 Zephyr path we like; (2) meter actual speaking seconds rather than session time (a future duration knob). Real logged usage is a small fraction of worst-case, so at expected P90 all tiers clear the <30% COGS bar comfortably — but the cap is what guarantees it.

E. Open questions — founder only

  1. Family voice scope. Recommended: Family includes everyday voice chat (default LoLo voice, daily-capped) and Plus adds the persona/voice packs. Confirm — or make voice Plus-only (one-rule change). The whole voice/persona split hinges on this.
  2. Voice metering unit. We can only cap number of STT/TTS calls today, not minutes. Accept the call-count mapping for launch, or fund a speaking-seconds duration knob so "15–20 min/day" is literally enforced? (Affects the honesty of the "healthy screen time" claim.)
  3. TTS model for margin. Keep the liked Gemini 3.1 Flash TTS / Zephyr ($20/1M) or move to Gemini 3.5 Flash TTS ($6/1M, ~70% cheaper) to widen voice margin? Trade-off is voice quality vs COGS.
  4. Support promise wording. "Email support from the makers" — acceptable, or drop the support bullet entirely?
  5. Post-trial lapse caps. The "tiny text-only free fallback" — confirm the exact numbers (proposed: text 10 msgs/day, 0 images, 0 voice, 1 child, read-only dashboard).
