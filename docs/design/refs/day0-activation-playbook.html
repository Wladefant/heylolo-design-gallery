HeyLolo Day-0 Activation Playbook

Launch Sept 8 · 14-day no-card trial · 55% of 3-day-trial cancels happen Day 0 · the first session decides everything. Research wave 2026-07-05 · lean brief (6 web sources + internal dossiers). Deep strategy lives in SYNTHESIS-WINNING-PLAYBOOK.md; this is the Day-0-specific execution layer.

0. Why Day 0 is the whole game (evidence)

  • Conversions cluster on Day 0. ~50.6% of all paid conversions happen Day 0 (RevenueCat State of Subscription Apps). Nearly all trial starts also happen Day 0 (78–90% across categories).
  • Cancels cluster on Day 0 too. 3-day trials: 55.4% of cancels are Day 0. Our 14-day trial: 35.7% of cancels are still Day 0 — the largest single bucket. The session that could convert is the same session that kills the trial.
  • Education is the hardest category. Education has the lowest Day-0 paid conversion (28.5% vs 71.9% Productivity) and low download-to-trial (6.5% median). Kids/edu buyers convert slowly and need proof — so Day 0's job is not "get the card," it's earn the parent's belief that this is worth keeping, then let the 14-day runway do the closing (17+ day trials convert 70% better than short ones — our long trial is an asset, not a liability).
  • The aha must land in session one. "The battle for the subscriber is won or lost in the first session." Over half of short-trial cancels happen within hours. If the magic moment doesn't happen in the first few minutes, it almost never happens.
  • The voice cold-start tax. ~20% of users of conversational agents leave without ever sending a first message (the "first-message barrier"). For a shy 6-year-old handed a talking disc, an open-ended "Hi, what do you want to talk about?" is a freeze trigger. Buddy.ai's whole moat is that kids talk to it "like a friend" — earned by a purpose-built child ASR + a proactive, scripted ice-breaker, never an open mic into silence.

The Day-0 thesis for HeyLolo: the aha moment is not a feature tour — it is the kid's own voice coming back answered by LoLo, and a wonder appearing on the shelf, witnessed by the parent, within the first ~3 minutes. Everything before that is friction to be cut; everything after is the seed for tomorrow.

1. The HeyLolo First Five Minutes — second-by-second spec

Three actors, one device, one continuous flow. The hard cut is parent setup ≤ 2 min → handoff → kid's first LoLo moment ≤ 3 min → parent sees the seed that evening.

Phase A — Parent onboarding, target < 2:00 (currently ~8 steps / "10/10")

The current child-profile flow is preset → general → avatar → interests → customInterests → supportAreas → chatStyle → pinCode (labelled up to 10/10). That is a 2–4 minute form before the kid ever hears LoLo. Cut it to the irreducible four:

t Step Keep / cut
0:00–0:20 Name + age (general) KEEP — age is the only input that changes LoLo's behavior on turn one.
0:20–0:45 Avatar (avatar) KEEP — 3-tap, it's the child's first ownership beat, and it's fun to do together.
0:45–1:20 Pick ONE thing you love (interests) KEEP but reduce to a single required tap (defaults to 1, "add more later"). This one tap is the ammunition for LoLo's ice-breaker.
1:20–2:00 Handoff primer card (new) KEEP — replaces the paywall/PIN wall as the last screen. "LoLo is awake and knows [Name] loves [dinosaurs]. Hand [Name] the phone — LoLo will say hi."

CUT from the Day-0 critical path (defer to a dashboard "Finish setting up" nudge, post-first-session):

  • customInterests, supportAreas, chatStyle → all have safe defaults (chatStyle already defaults to Friend; supportAreas already starts empty by design). None gate a good first conversation.
  • pinCode, quiet-hours-step, emergency-contact-step, phone-number-input → parental-control config, not first-session value. Move behind a "Secure your account" card on the dashboard shown after the kid's first session (parent is now motivated).

Rationale: every deferred step is a place a parent bounces before value. The 14-day trial buys us the runway to collect this later; Day 0 must reach the disc.

Phase B — The handoff moment (~1:20–2:00)

The single most under-designed moment in kids apps: parent-sets-up → kid-uses is usually a silent cliff. Make it a scripted baton pass:

  • The primer card names the child and the interest, and shows LoLo already awake and waiting (WAKE spring from the Daily Loop §3.1) — not a menu, not a "Start" button that dumps into an empty chat.
  • One line of parent instruction: "Hand [Name] the phone." No login wall, no "are you the parent?" gate between the card and the disc — that gate is what breaks the handoff.
  • Optional (age ≤ 6): a 4-second LoLo voice line plays on the card so the parent hears the character before handing over — sets the expectation and de-risks the mic.

Phase C — Kid's first LoLo moment, target first answer by ~2:30, first wonder by ~3:00

The ice-breaker must guarantee a kid answer — never open-ended. We already have the mechanism: seedConversationOpener (src/entities/ai-chat-conversation/api/seed-conversation-opener.ts) persists ONE assistant message with no round-trip. For Day 0, seed a First-Session Opener distinct from the generic QOTD:

  1. Wake + greet by name (0–5 s). LoLo wakes from the disc, says the child's name. Character-first, the interface is LoLo.
  2. The closed ice-breaker (5–15 s). Not "what do you want to talk about?" — a binary or pick-one question built from the interest tapped in setup, engineered so any answer is a win:
    • "I heard you love dinosaurs! Quick — would you rather meet a T-Rex or a Triceratops?"
    • The two named options are the escape hatch from the first-message freeze (ICEBREAKER "small, diverse starter list" pattern). A shy kid can whisper one word; a chatty kid can riff. Either way LoLo got a signal.
  3. Warm reaction + the first wonder (15–90 s). LoLo reacts specifically to the answer, then delivers ONE genuinely surprising, age-tuned fact/mini-story about their pick — the "magic moment" the whole trial hinges on. Keep it short (T1 ≈ one delightful beat).
  4. Permanence beat — the wonder flies to the shelf (~2:30–3:00). The fact mints a Wonder Shelf artifact (#142) visibly — the child watches it fly to the shelf. This is the Day-0 aha made tangible: they now own something, and it's a reason to come back tomorrow. Target: first shelf artifact earned within 3 minutes of handoff.
  5. Micro-close (optional, if the kid drifts). LoLo plants a tomorrow-seed ("Next time I'll show you the dino that was smaller than a chicken!") — promise-of-delight, never loss-framing.

Voice cold-start safeguards (Day-0-specific):

  • Never leave an open mic into silence. If no utterance in ~6 s, LoLo re-offers the same binary out loud ("You can just say T-Rex or Triceratops!") — one gentle re-prompt, then offers a tap fallback (the two options as tappable chips). A kid must never hit a dead mic on turn one.
  • Whisper-tolerant. Kid ASR is our known weak point (child WER 38–87%); on Day 0, bias the first turn toward the two expected keywords so a mumble still resolves to a valid branch rather than a "sorry, I didn't catch that" (the #1 confidence-killer for a shy first-timer).

Phase D — Parent sees the seed, same evening

The parent's Day-0 conversion signal is not a dashboard of metrics — it's a story. Wire the first session into Story of the Day (#166):

  • By evening the parent gets a push / dashboard card: "[Name] met LoLo today and earned their first wonder: why some dinosaurs had feathers. Ended calm. 🦕"
  • Leads with the shelf artifact + a process-praise line + an "ended calm" signal (never "12 minutes of screen time"). This is the emotional receipt that makes a parent think "okay, this is different — I'll keep it." — the actual Day-0 retention lever for an Education app that can't win on Day-0 card capture.

2. The 5 activation metrics to instrument (with targets)

Define "activated Day-0 account" = a child profile whose first session reached the first shelf artifact AND whose parent viewed the Story-of-the-Day seed. Track each sub-step so we can see where the 35.7% Day-0 cancels leak.

# Metric Definition Day-0 target
M1 Time-to-handoff profile-create-start → handoff primer card shown < 2:00 median (p75 < 3:00)
M2 Handoff-to-first-utterance rate kids who speak ≥1 word to LoLo / handoffs ≥ 80% (beats the ~20% first-message-barrier drop)
M3 Time-to-first-wonder handoff → first Wonder Shelf artifact minted < 3:00 median
M4 First-session completion sessions reaching ≥1 artifact + calm close (not rage-quit / dead-mic exit) ≥ 65% of started sessions
M5 Parent seed-view rate (Day 0/evening) parents who open the Story-of-the-Day card same day ≥ 50%; and Day-0 cancel rate < 30% (beat the 35.7% category baseline)

North-star to watch alongside: Day-0-activated → Day-14 trial-to-paid. The bet is that reaching M3 (first wonder) is the aha that predicts conversion; instrument the correlation from launch so we can prove/kill it.

The current 8-step ("10/10") child-profile form stands between install and the kid ever hearing LoLo. Issue B — feat(companion): First-Session Opener — closed ice-breaker that guarantees a first answer

~20% of conversational-agent users never send a first message; a shy child handed an open mic freezes. Add a Day-0 variant of seedConversationOpener that poses a binary/pick-one question built from the child's chosen interest + age tier ("would you rather meet a T-Rex or a Triceratops?"), reacts specifically, then delivers ONE surprising wonder that mints a visible Wonder-Shelf artifact within 3 min. Include a dead-mic safeguard (6-s → re-offer aloud + tap-chip fallback, ASR biased to the two keywords). Instrument M2/M3/M4. Files: src/entities/ai-chat-conversation/api/seed-conversation-opener.ts, src/widgets/conversation.

Issue C — feat(parent): Day-0 Story-of-the-Day seed — the first-session receipt

An Education app can't win Day 0 on card capture (28.5% category conversion); it wins by making the parent believe it's worth keeping during a 14-day trial that converts 70% better than short ones. Same evening as the first session, surface a Story-of-the-Day card: the wonder the child earned + "ended calm", never screen-time metrics. Instrument M5 (seed-view rate ≥ 50%, Day-0 cancel < 30%).
