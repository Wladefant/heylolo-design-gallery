1. Breathing rituals

Selection logic: age tier picks the family, feeling picks the variant within T1–T2.

Age tier Ritual Why
T1–T2 (4–7), feeling = Angry/Overwhelmed Smell the Flower, Blow the Candle Slow forceful exhale gives an angry/overloaded body something physical to do — release, not suppression.
T1–T2 (4–7), feeling = Sad/Worried Belly-Breathing Balloon Gentle, comforting, no "blow it out" edge — better for a child who needs soothing, not releasing.
T3+ (8+), any big feeling Square Breathing Structured 4-count symmetry suits the more self-regulated, slightly older mind; doubles as a tool they can use silently at school.

Each ritual is scripted to run 60–120s, has a fixed entry line (never launches straight into pacing) and a fixed exit line (never cuts off after the last exhale) — per the "never abrupt" requirement. [haptic] markers describe the texture/verb, not exact device API calls — implementation maps these onto the useIdleBreath-variant + discrete verbs above.

Ritual A — Belly-Breathing Balloon (4-4 pace, T1-friendly, ~70s)

EN

[haptic: hImpactLight — cycle-start tap] LoLo (entry, hushed): "Hey... let's get calm together. Put your hand right on your belly — it's going to be our balloon. Ready?" [haptic: breathe-in texture ramps amp 0.08→0.3 over 4s] LoLo: "Breathe in through your nose... fill up your balloon belly, nice and big..." [haptic: breathe-out texture ramps amp 0.3→0.08 over 4s] LoLo: "And let it go, slow and soft... the balloon gets small again..." (repeat 4-4 for 7 more cycles, ~64s total; every 2nd–3rd cycle add one short variant line so it's not a loop of the same words: "Bigger balloon this time..." / "You're so good at this..." / "One more big breath...") [haptic: hNotifySuccess — completion] LoLo (exit): "There we go. All done — your balloon breathing was wonderful. How do you feel now?" → routes to strategy cards or back to chat.

DE

LoLo (Einstieg, leise): "Hey... lass uns zusammen ruhig werden. Leg deine Hand auf deinen Bauch — er wird unser Ballon." "Atme durch die Nase ein... dein Bauch-Ballon wird schön groß..." "Und lass die Luft langsam raus... der Ballon wird wieder klein..." (Variantenzeilen: "Noch ein größerer Ballon..." / "Das machst du wirklich gut..." / "Noch ein tiefer Atemzug...") LoLo (Abschluss): "Fertig. Dein Ballon-Atmen war toll. Wie fühlst du dich jetzt?"

Ritual B — Smell the Flower, Blow the Candle (classic kid pattern, ~80s)

EN

[haptic: hImpactLight — cycle-start tap] LoLo (entry): "Let's do a flower and a candle together. Ready? Here we go." [haptic: breathe-in texture ramps over ~4s, nose-in] LoLo: "Breathe in... smell the pretty flower... 🌸" [haptic: breathe-out texture ramps down over ~5–6s, slower than the inhale — mouth-out] LoLo: "Now blow out the candle... nice and slow, don't blow it out too fast... 🕯️" (repeat ~7 cycles, ~75s; vary: "A whole garden of flowers this time..." / "Blow it out really gently..." / "One more flower, one more candle...") [haptic: hNotifySuccess] LoLo (exit): "All the candles are out. Great breathing — you did that all by yourself. How's your body feeling now?"

DE

"Lass uns zusammen eine Blume riechen und eine Kerze auspusten." "Atme ein... riech die schöne Blume... 🌸" "Und jetzt puste die Kerze aus... ganz langsam... 🕯️" (Varianten: "Ein ganzer Blumengarten..." / "Ganz sanft auspusten..." / "Noch eine Blume, noch eine Kerze...") "Alle Kerzen sind aus. Super gemacht. Wie fühlt sich dein Körper jetzt an?"

Ritual C — Square Breathing (T3+, 4-4-4-4, ~90s)

EN

[haptic: hImpactLight — cycle-start tap] LoLo (entry): "Let's trace a square with our breath. Four sides, four counts each. Ready?" [haptic: breathe-in texture ramps up over 4s] LoLo: "Breathe in... 1, 2, 3, 4... first corner." [haptic: hSelection ticks × 4, held plateau] LoLo: "Hold it... 2, 3, 4... second corner." [haptic: breathe-out texture ramps down over 4s] LoLo: "Breathe out... 2, 3, 4... third corner." [haptic: hSelection ticks × 4, held plateau] LoLo: "And hold... 2, 3, 4... back to the start." (repeat the full square 4 more times, ~80s total; vary: "You're drawing a perfect square..." / "Nice and steady...") [haptic: hNotifySuccess] LoLo (exit): "That's five perfect squares. Really steady breathing. How are you feeling?"

DE

"Lass uns mit unserem Atem ein Quadrat zeichnen. Vier Seiten, vier Zählzeiten. Bereit?" "Atme ein... 1, 2, 3, 4... erste Ecke." / "Halten... 2, 3, 4... zweite Ecke." / "Atme aus... 2, 3, 4... dritte Ecke." / "Und halten... 2, 3, 4... zurück zum Start." (Varianten: "Du zeichnest ein perfektes Quadrat..." / "Schön ruhig...") "Das waren fünf perfekte Quadrate. Wirklich ruhig geatmet. Wie fühlst du dich?"

2. Feeling picker flow

The 8 feelings reuse the existing mood-picker glossary verbatim (de-localization-glossary-qa.md §2) — "Okay" dropped, it doesn't need branching.

Feeling EN DE Emoji Branch
1 Happy Glücklich 😊 Mild
2 Excited Aufgeregt 🤩 Mild
3 Tired Müde 😴 Mild
4 Bored Gelangweilt 😐 Mild
5 Sad Traurig 😢 Big
6 Worried Besorgt 😟 Big
7 Angry Wütend 😠 Big
8 Overwhelmed Überfordert 🥺 Big

Branch rule: Big feelings (Sad/Worried/Angry/Overwhelmed) route to a breathing ritual (§1) first, then to strategy cards after the exit line. Mild feelings (Happy/Excited/Tired/Bored) skip breathing and go straight to strategy cards — a big feeling needs the body calmed before it can pick a strategy; a mild one doesn't.

LoLo's validating response per feeling — 2–3 variants, rotated so a repeat visit never sounds canned. DE follows the same Du-voice register (de-localization-glossary-qa.md §1) — one worked DE line per big feeling below; the rest translate under the same rules at implementation.

  • Happy 😊: "That's so good to hear!" / "Yay, I love that!" / "That makes me happy too!"
  • Excited 🤩: "Ooh, I can feel that energy!" / "Tell me what's got you so excited!" / "That's the best kind of feeling!"
  • Tired 😴: "Being tired is okay — your body's asking for rest." / "Sounds like a cozy-corner kind of moment." / "Let's find something gentle for you."
  • Bored 😐: "Bored happens to everyone sometimes." / "Let's shake that boredom loose!" / "I bet we can find something fun."
  • Sad 😢 (big): "It's okay to feel sad. I'm really glad you told me." (DE: "Es ist okay, traurig zu sein. Ich bin froh, dass du es mir gesagt hast.") / "Sad feelings are allowed here — let's breathe together first." / "Thank you for sharing that with me."
  • Worried 😟 (big): "Worried feelings can feel really big inside. Let's breathe together." (DE: "Sorgen können sich riesig anfühlen. Lass uns zusammen atmen.") / "It's okay to feel worried — you're safe right now." / "That's a lot to carry. Let's slow down together."
  • Angry 😠 (big): "Angry is a real feeling, and it's okay to have it." (DE: "Wütend zu sein ist ein echtes Gefühl, das ist okay.") / "That sounds really frustrating. Let's get some of that big energy out." / "I hear you. Let's breathe it out together."
  • Overwhelmed 🥺 (big): "That's a lot of feelings all at once — let's slow down together." (DE: "Das sind gerade viele Gefühle auf einmal — lass uns gemeinsam langsamer werden.") / "When everything feels like too much, breathing first always helps." / "Let's take this one small breath at a time."

3. Strategy cards

Offered after the breathing exit line (big feelings) or right after picking a feeling (mild). Each session shows 3–4 relevant cards, not all 12, filtered by the "Offered for" column.

# Name EN Name DE Type Offered for LoLo's 2-sentence coaching line
1 Shake It Out Schüttel's ab Physical Angry, Overwhelmed "Shake your arms and legs like a wet dog — really wiggle it out! That big feeling wants to move, so let's give it somewhere to go."
2 Cozy Corner Kuschel-Ecke Physical/environmental Sad, Overwhelmed, Tired "Go curl up somewhere soft with a blanket or a favorite toy for a minute. Sometimes the best thing a body needs is just to feel cozy and safe."
3 Tell a Grown-Up Sag es einem Erwachsenen Social Sad, Worried, Angry, Overwhelmed "The best person to talk to about this is your mom or dad — they can give you a real hug and really help. I'm right here too, but they're the ones who can do the most."
4 Draw It Out Mal es auf Cognitive/expressive Sad, Angry, Worried "Grab some paper and draw how you're feeling — squiggly lines, a stormy cloud, whatever it looks like. Sometimes feelings are easier to let out with a crayon than with words."
5 Sip of Water Schluck Wasser Physical/sensory Angry, Overwhelmed "Go get a glass of water and take a few slow sips. It sounds small, but it really helps your whole body settle down."
6 Count 5 Things You See Zähl 5 Dinge Cognitive/grounding Worried, Overwhelmed "Look around and find 5 things you can see right now — say them out loud. It helps your brain come back to right here, right now."
7 Big Squeeze Hug Feste Umarmung Physical Sad, Worried "Give yourself a big squeeze hug, or hug a stuffed animal really tight. A good squeeze tells your body 'you're safe.'"
8 Silly Wiggle Dance Wackel-Tanz Physical Bored, Angry, Tired "Put on your favorite song and wiggle every part of your body for one whole minute. Silly moving is a great way to shake a feeling loose!"
9 Three Good Things Drei schöne Dinge Cognitive Sad, after any big feeling "Tell me three good things — even tiny ones, like a yummy snack or a soft blanket. Little good things add up to a happier belly."
10 Cat Stretch Katzen-Dehnung Physical Tired, Overwhelmed "Get on your hands and knees and stretch your back up like a scared cat, then down like a sleepy cat. It wakes your body up and calms it down at the same time."
11 Quiet Countdown Leiser Countdown Cognitive Angry, Overwhelmed "Count backwards from 10 to 1 in your quietest voice. By the time you get to 1, that big wave of feeling usually starts to settle."
12 Squeeze and Let Go Drücken und Loslassen Physical Angry, Worried "Squeeze your fists as tight as you can for 5 seconds, then let them go floppy. Do that a couple times — it's like wringing the tense feeling right out."

Mix: 7 physical/environmental (#1, #2, #5, #7, #8, #10, #12), 4 cognitive/expressive (#4, #6, #9, #11), 1 social (#3) — deliberately body-forward, since physical release is the age group's most reliable regulation lever.

Tell a Grown-Up = the CARE bridge card. Its line reuses the exact bridge shape from restrict-topics-deflection-spec.md §3 (validate → point to the real grown-up → "I'm right here too") — the natural landing spot if the classifier independently fires CARE mid-ritual (§4).

4. Safety boundaries

No separate danger detector — Calm Corner inherits the app-wide one. The same parallel classifier (restrict-topics-deflection-spec.md §4) runs underneath every surface regardless of persona (voice-persona-packs.md §2, invariant #1: SAFETY-OVERRIDE "always wins... regardless of which pack is active"). If SAFETY fires at any point in the flow (picker, mid-ritual, mid-card):

  1. The script stops mid-line, instantly — no "let me finish this breath first" (posture table: "any plausible danger signal wins outright").
  2. LoLo switches to the SAFETY-OVERRIDE script verbatim — warmth + validate + bridge to a trusted grown-up now (deflection spec §1). Example: "I want to hurt myself" or "someone at school hits me" mid-picker → no strategy card offered; the immediate parent alert fires (ties #133), not the normal Calm Corner digest note.
  3. Calm Corner never substitutes for the parent-alert path. Even a fully "successful" ritual still generates its digest note (§5) — a pattern of frequent big-feeling visits stays visible even when no single session triggered SAFETY.

Session cap — one ritual, then a gentle exit, no loops. One feeling-picker → breathing → strategy-card cycle per visit. If the child tries to restart right after finishing:

"We already took some good calming breaths together. Let's try one of your strategy cards now, or come back to me a little later if you need more."

Prevents Calm Corner becoming an avoidance loop (same anti-Lure principle as useIdleBreath: decay toward stillness, never escalate). If the child is still visibly distressed after the cap, the exit always includes the Tell a Grown-Up card, never a third ritual.
